LEARN MORE ABOUT ME

Born and brought up in India, I moved at age 16 to join UWC ISAK Japan, an international boarding school in Japan. My journey as an artist however, started much later. At Carleton College, I indulged myself in film production and theory and focused on bringing South Asian narratives to the center stage.
The move to the US inspired me to draw on my own experiences as a third-culture child to create and express myself through an artistic outlet.I have worked in the public art industry and have collaborated with local artists and businesses to widen the range of my artistic vision. I have had the privilege of working with artists from all across the world and dared to pursue what I love. I am currently working on a documentary set in India and another set in Northfield, Minnesota

EXPERIENCE

I am involved with several public art projects and hope to engage in more such works in the future. I was the co-producer and project manager for The Wandering House in the summer of 2019. I really enjoy exploring different art platforms, even those outside the realm of media, and so in the summer of 2020, I will be delving into interior design when working with Annette English, an interior designer based in California. On campus, I am a student writer for The Carletonian, Carleton's premier student-run newspaper. I also write for other campus journals. Besides this, I am heavily involved with international student life on campus as a peer leader for the Office of Intercultural and International Life. Additionally, I possess immense experience in the fields of videography, video editing, photography, cinema studies, production and film history

Public Art

The Wandering House

The Wandering House is a public art installment. A former ice fishing house, retrofitted to become an audio recording studio, The Wandering House provides a reflective space for the members of Northfield community to explore their idea of home. Based in this small Midwestern town, this project is currently in post production and will be showcased at Imagining America in Albuquerque, New Mexico. For more information please visit this page.
